Lemon

The lemon is the sunny yellow delicacy that is said to have originally come from South Asia and Northeast India. Exactly where it first came from is unknown. The lemon is a hybrid of a sour orange and a pomelo. You can eat the whole lemon, but you usually remove the white layer between the yellow flesh and the outer peel as this can be very bitter and leave an unpleasent taste. The lemon grows on trees and requires a lot of heat and sun. It thrives in tropical countries but is also grown in Southern Europe. Lemons ripen all year around, but normally the citrus season is during our autumn and winter.
